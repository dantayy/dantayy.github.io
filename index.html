Playing hard by working hard...

Hi there! I'm Nick, an aspiring game designer who wishes to specialize in level design. Gaming's been a part of my life for as long as I can remember, and I've always wanted to give back to the industry that's given so much to me. Seeing as how I've always been proficient in math and science I decided pursuing a specialized degree in programming was the best way to achieve that goal and so now I'm here at the Rochester Institute of Technology. I've dabbled with basic coding in the past, but I'm learning more advanced things as you read this, and hope to have multiple functional games by the end of my time here.

At the moment I'm looking for a Summer 2019 co-op in the Game Design/Software Engineering/Web Development fields. Below is a collection of projects I've worked on pertaining to those fields, as well as some contact info. No matter how or why you ended up here, I hope you'll find something that piques your interest!

QuoteFault

Over the course of roughly one Spring semester, I built a quote-submission service for Computer Science House's weekly newsletter using Python packages such as Flask as well as a MySQL database. This was my first major personal coding project and it's currently being used by over 100 active members within the organization. It's still a work in progress, with multiple future improvements in the pipeline, as well as contributions from other members.

Sonagraphic

The idea for this game prototype came from the recurring thought of mine that my gaming music playlist should be more integrated into the games i was playing somehow. The hope is that by the end of the semester I'll be able to let the player grab music/audio from any video on YouTube (ala AudioShield) and then be dropped into an top-down, twin-stick shooter arena with waves of enemies coming at them that take damage based on how in-tune the player is making their shots with the music being played (ala Harmonix's cancelled Chroma). Further down the line I'd like to stylize it to look bright and gory (ala Hotline Miami). As of right now I've got a rudimentary prototype in Unreal, as well as a design document with more details on what the game should look/work like. My next step is porting the stuff I've learned from working in Unreal over to a Unity project, as I've realized there are some handy tools available in that engine that would greatly improve on what I've got so far.

Web Development

In addition to my work on Quotefault, in the Spring of 2018 I did a lot of smaller web-dev projects to cement my understanding of the skills. Most of that work can be found at my people.rit.edu page, and although I'd consider a lot of it to be rough around the edges, those projects helped me become confident enough in myself to put together the site you see before you today.
