Playing hard by working hard...

Hi there! I'm Nick, an aspiring game designer who wants to specialize in level design. Gaming's been a part of my life for as long as I can remember, and I've always wanted to give back to the industry that's given so much to me. After earning my degree in Game Design & Development at the Rochester Institute of Technology in the Spring of 2020, I landed a job at Lockheed Martin as a Software Engineering Associate.

At the moment I'm looking for a full-time position in the fields of Game Design, Software Engineering, and Web Development. No matter how or why you ended up here, I hope you'll find something that piques your interest!

Odds Bot

In the Sring of 2020 I learned how to program a Slack bot that would allow users to virtually challenge each other in a simple game of Odds. Leveraging a redis database on the backend to store and maintain challenges, this was a fun and lightweight venture into Node.js app development! The bot works great in the app, and can be cloned from github to be hosted for your own org with relative ease if you follow the setup guide.

Sleep Patrol Alpha Level Design

Early in 2021 I was asked by a friend to assist with creating some new content for their group's mobile platforming game. I collaborated with the team to figure out what would work best for a second level in their game and applied my level design know-how and feedback from them to iterate on layouts to produce a final product that should be hitting app stores in an update soon

Quotefault

Over the course of the Spring of 2017, I built a quote-submission service for Computer Science House's weekly newsletter using Python packages such as Flask as well as a MySQL database. This was my first major personal coding project and it's currently being used by over 100 active members within the organization. It's still a work in progress, with multiple future improvements in the pipeline, as well as contributions from other members.
